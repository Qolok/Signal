# The Story

You are the crew of the Endymion 7, a deep-range mining vessel. Your ship has crash-landed on an uncharted alien planet. You don't know what brought you down. You don't know what's out there. You have what survived the crash, and each other — for now.

Somewhere out there, scattered across the terrain, are pieces of your radio. Find them and bring them back to the Signal Array. Rescue is possible but not guaranteed. And the longer you're stranded, the more the planet changes you.

## The Three Tensions

- **Survival.** Rations and O2 Tanks deplete every round. The crash site is your lifeline. Venture too far and you may not make it back.
- **Exploration.** Radio Fragments are out there, but finding them means pushing deeper into unknown terrain — away from safety, away from others, into situations only you will know about.
- **Trust.** Corruption changes a player's win condition silently. The group must cooperate to survive, but cooperation requires trust the planet is quietly eroding.

# Setup

1. Place the Crash Site tile at the center of the table.
2. Shuffle all Terrain tiles, Anomaly tiles, and Ship Section tiles together face-down into one draw stack.
3. Shuffle the Event deck. Shuffle the Equipment deck separately.
4. Each player places their pawn on the Crash Site hex.
5. Each player draws 3 Equipment cards.
6. Each player takes 10 Ration tokens (all FULL), 3 O2 Tank tokens (all FULL), and 3 Health tokens (all HEALTHY).
7. Roll to see who goes first.

# Base Camp

Base Camp is a 7-hex cluster — your anchor point on the planet. O2 Tanks do **not** deplete when you end a turn on any Base Camp tile.

The layout of Base Camp is chosen by players during setup — the six surrounding modules can be arranged in any connected shape around the central Crash Site hex.

| Location | Effect |
|---|---|
| Crash Site | All players begin here. No specific action — this is your anchor point. |
| Medical Bay | Action: Restore 1 Health token to HEALTHY. |
| Equipment Locker | Action: Draw 1 Equipment card. One draw per turn maximum. |
| Cargo Hold | Action: Deposit or withdraw Rations from communal storage. |
| Signal Array | Action: Activate Radio Fragments and roll for rescue. Capacity: 1 player only. |
| Watch Tower | Action: Reveal all face-down tiles adjacent to any crew member currently in the field. |
| Airlock | Passive: Refill 1 O₂ Tank automatically when returning from terrain. |

**Signal Array:** Fits one player at a time. While occupied, no other player may enter. The occupant decides each round whether to roll — they are never required to. If another player wants the occupant out, both roll 1 die and keep rolling until one wins.

**Airlock:** The O₂ refill triggers only when entering from outside Base Camp (returning from terrain). Moving between Base Camp hexes does not trigger it.

**Watch Tower:** Reveals the fog of war around your scouts without requiring anyone to be in danger. The more crew in the field, the more tiles revealed.

# Terrain & Tiles

All terrain tiles begin face-down in a single shuffled stack. The map expands outward from Base Camp as players explore. There are 50 tiles total: 40 terrain, 7 anomaly, and 3 ship sections — all shuffled together.

**Frontier Rule:** The play area is always wrapped in face-down tiles. Every hex adjacent to any revealed tile that does not yet have a tile is immediately filled with a face-down tile from the deck. This happens automatically — the board stays wrapped in mystery until the deck runs out.

**Entering a Face-Down Tile:** When a player moves onto a face-down tile, flip it immediately. Its contents are now visible to everyone.

## Points of Interest

Each terrain tile contains one or more Points of Interest. POIs are where events are drawn and resources found. Entering a new tile triggers an Event card draw automatically.

| POI | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Signal Tower | A broadcasting structure, origin unknown. May contain a Radio Fragment. |
| Fuselage | Hull section from the Endymion 7. Still holds cargo. |
| Cave | Natural shelter. Atmospheric sensors can't reach inside. |
| Outpost | A supply depot, damaged but stocked. |
| Wreckage Field | Debris scattered across the terrain — hazardous, but potentially useful. |
| Recovered Terminal | A functional console, still drawing power. The data is yours alone. |

## Anomaly Tiles

When a player enters a tile and flips it to find an Anomaly, the effect takes place immediately.

| Anomaly | Rule |
|---|---|
| Stasis Pod | You may enter. Turn pawn sideways. Skip Resource Flip each round inside. Cannot move or interact. Exit any time on your turn. |
| Temporal Rift | Roll 1 die. 1–3: flip that many Rations to EMPTY. 4–6: flip that many EMPTY Rations to FULL. |
| Portal | You may immediately move your pawn to the Crash Site. Your turn ends. You may decline. |
| Gravitational Well | Roll 1 die. The player to your left moves your pawn that many hexes in any direction. |
| Dead Signal | No Signal Roll occurs this round for any player. |
| Echo Chamber | Resolve the most recent Public Event card again in full. |
| Inversion Field | Choose any other player. Swap your Ration tokens with theirs. They cannot refuse. |

## Ship Section Tiles

Three Ship Section tiles are shuffled into the terrain deck. Indistinguishable from terrain until flipped. When found, they function as a secondary refuge.

| Location | Effect |
|---|---|
| Airlock | Refill 1 O2 Tank to FULL. |
| Emergency Bay | Restore 1 Health token to HEALTHY. |
| Supply Cache | Roll 1 die and flip that many EMPTY Rations to FULL. |

Ship Sections do not contain a Signal Array. Radio Fragments cannot be activated here. They are refuge, not rescue.

# Taking a Turn

On your turn, do three things — in any order that makes sense:

**Move once.** Roll 1 die and move that many hexes. You may stop short.

**Take actions.** As many as you like: trade resources, draw Equipment, use the Signal Array, or use any Base Camp facility. Each action is available only at the appropriate location.

**Flip your resources.** When done, flip 1 Ration to EMPTY. If not on Base Camp, flip 1 O2 Tank to EMPTY. This is always last.

**Trading:** Resources and Equipment cards may be traded only with players sharing your current tile. The WALKIE extends this to an adjacent tile.

**Investigating a POI:** Entering a new tile triggers an Event card draw automatically. Draw the top card and resolve it. Public cards are read aloud. Private cards 🔒 are read silently and kept to yourself unless you choose to share.

## The Signal Roll

At any point during their turn, a player at the Signal Array may choose to roll all 3 dice. They are never required to do so. The roll may also be attempted at the start of a turn, before moving.

The combined total must meet or exceed the threshold:

| Fragments Activated | Roll This or Higher |
|---|---|
| 0 | No roll possible |
| 1 | 18 |
| 2 | 16 |
| 3 | 14 |
| 4 | 12 |
| 5 (maximum) | 10 |

If the roll succeeds, rescue arrives. All surviving players whose win condition is rescue win the game.

# Resources

**Rations:** 10 per player. One flipped to EMPTY each turn regardless of location. Always visible — no player may conceal how many they have. Cache yield: roll 1 die, flip that many EMPTY to FULL. Surplus beyond 10 may be shared immediately or is lost.

**O2 Tanks:** 3 per player. One flipped to EMPTY each turn spent away from Base Camp. Returning to Base Camp refills all O2 Tanks to FULL. **Finite — no new O2 Tanks enter the game.** Can only be redistributed via trading.

**Health:** 3 tokens per player. Lost through starvation, certain events, and conflict.

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| 0 Rations at Resource Flip | Flip 1 Health to WOUNDED every turn. |
| 0 O2 Tanks away from Base Camp | Flip 1 Health to WOUNDED every turn. |
| All 3 Health WOUNDED | INCAPACITATED. |
| 2 rounds INCAPACITATED | DEAD. |

**Incapacitation:** Cannot move or act. May be saved if another player reaches their tile and donates at least 1 O2 Tank before their next turn begins.

**Death:** Everything they were carrying — Rations, O2 Tanks, Equipment cards, Radio Fragments — is left at their current location. A dead player is out of the game. Their secrets die with them.

# Radio Fragments

There are 5 Radio Fragment tokens in the game. They represent pieces of the ship's emergency transmitter, scattered across the terrain by the crash.

Fragments are found at Signal Tower tiles and through Event cards. When discovered, the finding player carries the token. To contribute to rescue, a Fragment must be brought to the Signal Array at Base Camp and activated there.

Each activated Fragment lowers the threshold required to trigger rescue. If a player carrying a Fragment dies, the token is left at their location for others to retrieve.

# Corruption

Some Event cards are Corruption cards. A player who draws one does not announce it. Nothing about them changes visibly.

> *Corrupted win condition: Prevent rescue from triggering until all other players are dead or incapacitated. You do not want to leave.*

A corrupted player may do anything the rules permit. Hold a Radio Fragment without returning it. Occupy the Signal Array without rolling. Decline to share resources. The planet does not require them to explain themselves.

**Conflict Resolution:** Can't agree on a decision? Roll for it. Each player rolls 1 die. Keep rolling until someone wins.

# Winning and Losing

| Condition | Result |
|---|---|
| Signal Roll meets or exceeds threshold | Rescue arrives. All surviving players whose goal is rescue win. |
| All rescue-seeking players are dead | The corrupted player wins. The planet keeps everyone else. |
| All players are dead | Nobody wins. |

# Event Cards

The Event deck is drawn whenever a player enters a new terrain tile. Cards are independent of the terrain — the tile tells you where you are; the card tells you what happens.

Cards are either **Public** (read aloud to the table) or **Private 🔒** (read silently — keep to yourself or show only to crew on your tile).

Some cards have immediate mechanical effects. Others are purely narrative — information only the drawing player receives.

## Sample Cards

> **[PUBLIC] Emergency supplies found, partially intact.**
> Roll 1 die for Ration yield.

> **[PUBLIC] Salvageable material nearby.**
> Roll 1 die: 1–3 = nothing; 4–5 = 1 Ration; 6 = Equipment card.

> **[PUBLIC] A sealed equipment cache. Contents intact.**
> Draw 1 Equipment card.

> **[PUBLIC] A Radio Fragment — yours, ejected on impact. Still functional.**
> Take 1 Radio Fragment token.

> **[PUBLIC] Natural shelter here.**
> The terrain blocks atmospheric sensors. Skip your O₂ flip this round.

> **[PUBLIC] Structural instability.**
> The ground shifts underfoot. Flip 1 Ration to EMPTY.

> **[PUBLIC] Nothing here.**
> Whatever this place held has been taken by the terrain.

> **[PUBLIC] Signs of prior occupation — too recent to be from the crash.**
> Someone else was here.

> **[PRIVATE 🔒] You find something personal.**
> A crew member's effects, separated from the wreck. You keep it to yourself.

> **[PRIVATE 🔒] A data chip, still readable.**
> The contents are for your eyes only.

> **[PRIVATE 🔒] Something moves at the edge of your vision.**
> You say nothing to the others.

> **[PRIVATE 🔒] A hidden cache — stowed deliberately, not by the crash.**
> Draw 1 Equipment card. You pocket the contents before anyone else arrives.

> **[PRIVATE 🔒] A partial crew log from before the crash.**
> Someone knew this was coming. You now know something the others don't.

# Solo Play

Remove all Corruption cards from the Event deck. Draw 2 Event cards per investigation instead of 1. All other rules apply unchanged.

You win if rescue arrives before your resources run out. You lose when your resources run out.

# Scenario Cards (Optional)

Once familiar with the base game, shuffle the Scenario cards and draw one before setup. Place it face-up at the center of the table. Read the backstory aloud. The single rule at the bottom is the only change to the base game.

## Lost Cargo

*The cargo bay separated on impact and came down somewhere else. Nobody saw where. The survival equipment, the tools, the gear you trained with — all of it is in a section of the ship you haven't found yet.*

> **RULE:** Players draw no Equipment cards at setup. Equipment cards may only be found at Ship Section tiles and the Equipment Locker.

## Thin Atmosphere

*The planet's atmosphere is technically breathable. What the readings didn't capture is how thin it is up here, how little each breath actually gives you.*

> **RULE:** O2 Tanks deplete 2 per round away from Base Camp instead of 1.

## Extreme Gravity

*Nobody checked the gravity index before descent. Now every step costs twice what it should. Moving across the terrain is exhausting in a way that compounds with every hour.*

> **RULE:** Halve your movement roll, rounded up.

## Hostile Terrain

*The surface looks stable. It isn't. Every time you stop to investigate something, the planet takes a little more from you. Nothing out here is free.*

> **RULE:** Investigating any POI costs 1 Ration in addition to normal effects.
