# SIGNAL

*A game of survival, exploration, and eroding trust — where rescue is uncertain, and the greatest threat may be sitting across the table.*

**1–6 Players · Ages 14+ · 45–90 Minutes**

---

# The Story

You are the crew of the Endymion 7, a deep-range mining vessel. Your ship has crash-landed on an uncharted alien planet. You don't know what brought you down. You don't know what's out there. You have what survived the crash, and each other — for now.

Somewhere out there, scattered across the terrain, are pieces of your radio. Find them and rebuild the Signal Array. Rescue is possible but not guaranteed. And the longer you're stranded, the more the planet changes you.

---

# How to Play

Signal is a survival game. All players begin at a crashed ship with nothing but a handful of equipment and a dwindling supply of food and oxygen. The planet around you is unmapped — you reveal it as you explore, placing tiles as you go.

Your goal is to locate Radio Fragments scattered across the terrain, return them to the ship's Signal Array, and activate a rescue beacon strong enough to be heard. Rescue arrives when the Signal Roll succeeds. Anyone alive at that moment wins.

But survival is not just about resources and navigation. The planet corrupts. Some players may find themselves — quietly, privately — working against the group.

The game has no fixed end point. You don't know when rescue will come. You don't know who, if anyone, has been corrupted. You only know what you can see, what you've been told, and how much you have left.

## The Three Tensions

- **Survival.** Rations and O2 Tanks deplete every round. The crash site is your lifeline. Venture too far and you may not make it back.
- **Exploration.** Radio Fragments are out there, but finding them means pushing deeper into unknown terrain — away from safety, away from others, into situations only you will know about.
- **Trust.** Corruption changes a player's win condition silently. The group must cooperate to survive, but cooperation requires trust the planet is quietly eroding.

Signal is designed to teach itself with instructions revealed through gameplay. The only things players need to know before their first turn:

- Roll to move.
- Draw and place tiles when you reach unexplored space.
- Stop at a Point of Interest to investigate and draw an Event card.
- Flip your resources when your turn ends.
- Survive until rescue.

## Setup

1. Place the Crash Site tile at the center of the table.
2. Shuffle all Terrain tiles and Anomaly tiles together face-down into one draw stack.
3. Shuffle the Event deck. Shuffle the Equipment deck separately.
4. Each player places their pawn on the Core Wreckage hex of the Crash Site.
5. Each player draws 3 Equipment cards.
6. Each player takes 10 Ration tokens (all FULL), 3 O2 Tank tokens (all FULL), and 3 Health tokens (all HEALTHY).
7. Roll to see who goes first.

# Components

## Tiles

- **1 Crash Site tile** — A large 7-hex cluster representing the wreckage of your ship. This tile is always placed at the center of the table at the start of the game. It contains named locations that players can visit and use throughout play.
- **40 Terrain tiles** — These form the planet around the Crash Site. All terrain tiles begin face-down in a shuffled stack. Reveal unexplored terrain to recover Radio Fragments and supply caches. But be careful as you may encounter unexpected anomalies along the way.
- **3 Ship Section tiles** — Shuffled into the terrain deck, Ship Sections are fragments of your vessel that survived the crash and landed elsewhere on the planet. When flipped, they function as a secondary refuge — players can refill O2 Tanks, restore Health, and find Ration caches there. They do not have a Signal Array. Radio Fragments cannot be activated at a Ship Section. Finding one deep in the terrain can change the entire shape of a game.

## The Event Deck

The Event deck is the voice of the planet. It is a single shuffled deck of 80 cards drawn whenever a player investigates a Point of Interest. Each card either resolves a moment in the game — a discovery, a danger, a complication — or delivers private information that only the drawing player (and anyone with them) receives.

Cards are marked with their visibility:

- **Public** — Show or read aloud to all players at the table.
- **Private 🔒** — Read silently. Show only to players on your tile, or keep to yourself if alone.

A private card shared between players on the same tile is passed physically — show the card to your hexmates, then return it to the discard pile. The rest of the table can see that something was shown. They cannot see what it was.

**Disinformation cards** instruct the drawing player to deceive the group in a specific way.

**Expedition cards** mandate that a portion of the group immediately travels somewhere, splitting the party.

**Corruption cards** shift a player's win condition without any announcement, rule change, or visible effect. The game continues normally, but one or more of the crew is keeping secrets.

## Radio Fragments

There are 5 Radio Fragment tokens in the game. They represent pieces of the ship's emergency transmitter, scattered across the terrain by the crash.

Fragments are found at Points of Interest and through certain Event cards. When discovered, the finding player carries the token physically — it sits in front of them on the table. Carrying a Fragment has no mechanical effect. To contribute to rescue, a Fragment must be brought back to the Signal Array at the Crash Site and activated there.

Each activated Fragment lowers the threshold the group must roll to trigger rescue. The more Fragments activated, the better the odds — up to a maximum of 5, which gives the group a 50% chance per round. Rescue is never guaranteed.

If a player carrying a Fragment dies, the token is left at their location for others to retrieve.

## Equipment Cards

30 Equipment cards are shuffled into their own deck. Each player draws 3 at the start of the game — these are the tools that survived the crash. More can be found at the Equipment Locker on the Crash Site and through certain Event cards.

Equipment cards are kept in front of each player and may be traded with players on the same tile during a turn. Each card explains its own effect.

## Tokens

- **Ration tokens (10 per player)** — Double-sided: FULL and EMPTY. One is flipped each round. Found in terrain caches.
- **O2 Tank tokens (3 per player)** — Double-sided: FULL and EMPTY. One is flipped to EMPTY each turn spent away from the Crash Site. Refilled by entering the Crash Site or a Ship Section. Finite — no new O2 Tanks enter the game. They can only be traded between players.
- **Health tokens (3 per player)** — Double-sided: HEALTHY and WOUNDED. Lost through starvation, certain events, or conflict.

## Dice

3 standard six-sided dice serve all purposes in the game: movement, the Signal Roll, Anomaly effects, conflict resolution, and cache yields. You never need more than three at once.

# The Board

## The Crash Site

The Crash Site is a 7-hex cluster — your main base of operations. Refill all O2 Tanks by ending a turn here. The Crash Site contains six named locations:

| Location | What It Does |
|---|---|
| Core Wreckage | All players begin here. |
| Medical Bay | Restore 1 Health token to HEALTHY as an action. |
| Equipment Locker | Draw 1 Equipment card as an action. Equipment found during exploration is deposited here. |
| Cargo Hold | Communal Ration storage. Deposit or withdraw freely as an action. |
| Signal Array* | Activate Radio Fragments and roll for rescue. Capacity: 1 player only (see below). |
| Landing Struts (×2) | The two outer entry and exit hexes connecting the Crash Site to the wider map. |

*The Signal Array fits one player at a time. While occupied, no other player may enter. The occupant decides each round whether to roll the Signal dice. They are never required to roll.

- If no player is present, no Signal Roll occurs.
- If another player wants the occupant out, they may contest it: both roll 1 die, keep rolling until one wins. The winner controls the array.

## Terrain Tiles

All terrain tiles begin face-down in a single shuffled stack. The map is built outward from the Crash Site as players explore. Every tile is identical on its back — there is no way to know what a tile contains until a crew member is placed on it and it is flipped.

**Placing Tiles**

Players move from hex corner point to hex corner point. Each corner point is shared by three hexes. When a player's movement brings their pawn to a corner point where one or two of those three hexes have no tile yet, they immediately draw and place tiles face-down to fill those empty slots. This happens as part of movement — it costs no additional points. The board grows naturally as players explore the frontier.

**Entering a Face-Down Tile**

After filling empty slots at a corner point, the player may spend 1 movement point to move their pawn into one of the newly placed tiles. Flip that tile face-up the moment the pawn is placed on it. Its internal paths and Points of Interest are now visible to everyone.

The crew member is placed at the nearest Point of Interest on the tile.

## Points of Interest

Each terrain tile contains 2–4 Points of Interest connected by paths. POIs are the locations where things happen — where events are drawn, resources found, and Fragments discovered. Passing through a tile without stopping at a POI triggers nothing.

To investigate a POI, a player must stop there and choose to investigate. This ends their movement for the turn and triggers an Event card draw.

| POI Type | What May Be Found |
|---|---|
| Fuselage | Equipment cards, possibly a Radio Fragment |
| Cave | Shelter — skip O2 Tank flip this round if investigated |
| Outpost | Ration cache (roll 1 die for yield) |
| Signal Tower | Radio Fragment |
| Wreckage Field | Random salvage — roll on the Event deck |
| Dead End | Nothing visible. Draw an Event card regardless. |

## Anomaly Tiles

When a player enters a tile and flips it to find an Anomaly, the printed rule on that tile takes effect immediately and applies to all players occupying it.

| Anomaly | Rule |
|---|---|
| Stasis Pod | You may enter. Turn pawn sideways. Skip Resource Flip each round inside. Cannot move or interact. Exit any time on your turn. |
| Temporal Rift | Roll 1 die. 1–3: flip that many Rations to SPENT. 4–6: flip that many EMPTY Rations to FULL. |
| Portal | You may immediately move your pawn to the Crash Site. Your turn ends. You may decline. |
| Gravitational Well | Roll 1 die. The player to your left moves your pawn that many points in any direction. |
| Dead Signal | No Signal Roll occurs this round for any player. |
| Echo Chamber | Resolve the most recent Public Event card again in full. |
| Inversion Field | Choose any other player. Swap your Ration tokens with theirs. They cannot refuse. |

## Ship Section Tiles

Three Ship Section tiles are shuffled into the terrain deck before play. They are indistinguishable from terrain tiles until flipped. When a crew member enters one and flips it, they find a fragment of the crashed ship — a secondary refuge in the wilderness.

| Location | What It Does |
|---|---|
| Airlock | Refill all your O2 Tanks to FULL. |
| Emergency Bay | Restore 1 Health token to HEALTHY every round spent in the bay. |
| Supply Cache | Roll 1 die and flip that many EMPTY Rations to FULL. |

Ship Sections do not contain a Signal Array. Radio Fragments cannot be activated here. They are refuge, not rescue.

Their positions on the board are random every game. A Ship Section found close to the Crash Site is a modest convenience. One discovered deep in dangerous terrain changes what is possible entirely.

# Taking a Turn

On your turn, you do three things — in any order that makes sense:

- **Move once.** Roll 1 die and move that many points along hex sides. You may stop short. Movement is explained in full under The Board.
- **Take actions.** You may take as many actions as you like: trade resources, draw Equipment, refill O2 Tanks, activate the Signal Array, investigate a POI, or any combination. Each action is available only at the appropriate location.
- **Flip your resources.** When you are done, flip 1 Ration to EMPTY and — if you are not on the Crash Site — flip 1 O2 Tank to EMPTY. This is always the last thing you do.

## Trading

Resources and Equipment cards may be traded only with players sharing your current tile.

The WALKIE is an Equipment card that extends this range — it lets you share information and resources with a player on an adjacent tile. Who you give it to (and when) is one of the most loaded decisions in the game.

## Investigating a POI

When you stop at a Point of Interest and choose to investigate, draw the top card of the Event deck. Read it according to its visibility:

- **Public cards** — Show or read aloud to all players at the table.
- **Private cards (🔒)** — Read silently. Show only to players on your tile, or keep to yourself if alone. A private card shared between players on the same tile is passed physically — show the card to your hexmates, then return it to the discard pile.

## The Signal Roll

At any point during their turn, a player occupying the Signal Array may choose to roll all 3 dice for rescue. They are never required to do so.

The combined total must meet or exceed the threshold for the current number of activated Radio Fragments:

| Radio Fragments Activated | Roll This or Higher to Trigger Rescue |
|---|---|
| 0 | No roll possible |
| 1 | 18 |
| 2 | 16 |
| 3 | 14 |
| 4 | 12 |
| 5 (maximum) | 10 |

If the roll succeeds, rescue arrives. All surviving players whose win condition is rescue win the game. The Signal Roll may only be attempted once per turn.

# Resources

## Rations

Each crew member starts with 10 Rations. One is flipped to EMPTY at the end of every turn, regardless of location. Ration tokens are always visible — no player may conceal how many they have.

Ration caches are found at Outpost POIs and through certain Event cards. Roll 1 die for the cache yield. Flip that many EMPTY tokens back to FULL. Any surplus beyond 10 may be shared with crew members on your tile immediately or is lost.

A player with 0 Rations flips 1 Health token to WOUNDED at the end of their Resource Flip instead.

## O2 Tanks

Each player starts with 3 O2 Tanks. One is flipped to EMPTY at the end of any turn spent away from the Crash Site. Returning to the Crash Site and ending a turn there refills all O2 Tanks to FULL.

O2 Tanks are finite. No new tokens enter the game. They can only be redistributed between players during trading. A player may give any number of FULL or EMPTY O2 Tanks to a player on their tile.

Lending O2 Tanks is a social contract. The game does not track debts or enforce returns. A player who accepts borrowed O2 Tanks and does not give them back has done nothing against the rules.

## Health

Each player starts with 3 Health tokens. Health is lost through starvation, certain events, and conflict.

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| 0 Rations at Resource Flip | Flip 1 Health to WOUNDED every turn. |
| 0 O2 Tanks at Resource Flip, away from Crash Site | Flip 1 Health to WOUNDED every turn. |
| All 3 Health tokens WOUNDED | You are INCAPACITATED. |
| 2 rounds of INCAPACITATION | You are DEAD. |

## Incapacitation

A player who runs out of Health tokens while away from the Crash Site is incapacitated. They cannot move or act on their next turn. After 2 turns of incapacitation, the crew member dies (see Death).

They may be saved if another player reaches their tile and donates at least 1 O2 Tank before the incapacitated player's turn begins. Both players must then return to the Crash Site to refill.

What the rescued player does with any borrowed O2 Tanks after that is their choice.

## Death

A player who has been INCAPACITATED for 2 rounds dies. Everything they were carrying — Rations, O2 Tanks, Equipment cards, Radio Fragments — is left at their current location. Other players may collect these by visiting the tile.

A dead player is out of the game. Their secrets die with them.

---

# Corruption

Some Event cards are Corruption cards. A player who draws one does not announce it. Nothing about them changes visibly. They simply now have a different reason to be here.

> *Corrupted win condition: Prevent rescue from triggering until all other players are dead or incapacitated. You do not want to leave.*

A corrupted player may do anything the rules permit. Hold a Radio Fragment without returning it. Occupy the Signal Array without rolling. Decline to share resources. Steer the group toward danger. The planet does not require them to explain themselves.

The Event deck contains a fixed number of Corruption cards, shuffled in before play. They may surface early or late — or never be drawn at all. The threat exists regardless.

## Conflict Resolution

> *Can't agree on a decision? Roll for it. Each player rolls 1 die. Keep rolling until someone wins.*

---

# Winning and Losing

| Condition | Result |
|---|---|
| Signal Roll meets or exceeds threshold | Rescue arrives. All surviving players whose goal is rescue win. |
| All rescue-seeking players are dead | The corrupted player wins. The planet keeps everyone else. |
| All players are dead | Nobody wins. |

# Scenario Cards (Optional)

Once you are familiar with the base game, shuffle the Scenario cards and draw one before setup for advanced play. Place it face-up at the center of the table. Read the backstory aloud. The single rule at the bottom is the only change to the base game. Everything else plays as normal.

Scenario cards are recommended for players who have completed at least one full game.

## Lost Cargo

*The cargo bay separated on impact and came down somewhere else. Nobody saw where. The survival equipment, the tools, the gear you trained with — all of it is in a section of the ship you haven't found yet. You have your suits and each other. Everything else has to be earned. Whether you share what you find is up to you.*

> **RULE:** Players draw no Equipment cards at setup. Equipment cards may only be found at Ship Section tiles and the Equipment Locker at the Crash Site.

## Thin Atmosphere

*The planet's atmosphere is technically breathable — the readings said so before you came down. What the readings didn't capture is how thin it is up here, how little each breath actually gives you. Your O2 Tanks are burning faster than projected. Every expedition needs to be shorter, every scout needs more support, and the distance between the crash site and any Radio Fragment suddenly feels a lot longer than it looked on the map.*

> **RULE:** O2 Tanks deplete 2 per round away from the Crash Site instead of 1.

## Extreme Gravity

*Nobody checked the gravity index before descent. Why would they — it was a routine survey. Now every step costs twice what it should. Equipment feels like it weighs three times as much. Moving across the terrain is exhausting in a way that compounds with every hour. The planet isn't hostile exactly. It's just indifferent to how hard it is to exist here.*

> **RULE:** Halve your movement roll, rounded up.

## Hostile Terrain

*The surface looks stable. It isn't. The terrain on this planet is corrosive, unstable, and actively consuming anything that moves across it. Suits degrade. Boots dissolve. Every time you stop to investigate something, the planet takes a little more from you. Nothing out here is free. Every discovery has a cost.*

> **RULE:** Investigating any POI costs 1 Ration in addition to normal effects.

---

# Solo Play

Remove all Corruption cards from the Event deck. Draw 2 Event cards per investigation instead of 1. All other rules apply unchanged.

You win if rescue arrives before your resources run out. You lose when your resources run out.
